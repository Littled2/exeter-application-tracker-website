How to Write a Killer CV

1. Start with a Strong Opening

Kick off with a brief personal statement that captures who you are and what you bring to the table. Keep it focused and relevant to the role.

2. Tailor It to the Job

Adjust your CV for each application, highlighting the skills and experiences that match the job description. Use keywords the employer is looking for.

3. Show Your Impact

Instead of listing duties, focus on achievements. Use numbers to back up your successes, like “boosted sales by 15%” or “managed a team of five.”

4. Keep It Simple and Clean

Use a clear, professional layout with easy-to-read fonts and enough white space. Make sure your CV is well-organized and visually appealing.

5. Proofread

Double-check for typos and grammatical errors. A polished CV shows you care about details and professionalism.
